November 30, 2014 update

Over 60 teams from around the globe have submitted their assignment in the inbox of pupc.submit@gmail.com. Thank you, everyone, for your hard work! The submissions will be judged within one month, and results will be announced before the end of December!
You may now also access the Online Part problem packets from PUPC-2014, in which over 60 teams from approx. 20 countries competed: Condensed Matter packet and Cosmology packet.

Results: Onsite

40 students have competed in our inaugural on-campus part of the Princeton University Physics Competition on Saturday, November 22, 2014! The rankings are now available here.
You may now also access the problem packets that you were given: full version and short version.
We encourage everyone who attended to please fill out a feedback form ASAP, which has been sent to all contestants through e-mail (if you have checked in onsite without pre-registration and have not given us your e-mail address, please send a message to psps@princeton.edu, and we will e-mail you the Feedback form).
Thank you very much and we hope to see you, along with your other schoolmates and friends, next year!
