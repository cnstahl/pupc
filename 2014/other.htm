Schedule for Online Part

  • 0:00 - 0:30 am EST November 15, 2014: The team-based Online Part is released. Teams will have one week to work on this part, choosing one or both of two available topics.
  • 0:30 am EST November 24, 2014: The Online Part is due, to be submitted as specified in the rules (this includes the time extension). You may submit later, but we cannot guarantee consideration.
  • The Online Part will be judged separately for a duration of several weeks.

    Schedule for Onsite Test

  • Noon on November 22, 2014: Arrival of contestants starts
  • 12:00 - 1:30 pm: Registration in Jadwin Hall 111 (the 'Fishbowl' room).
  • 12:30 - 1:30 pm: An informal meet-and-greet with lunch served in the Jadwin Hall lobby in front of the Joseph Henry Room. (Registration continues in room 111; signs will be posted.)
  • 2:00 - 4:00 pm: The individual Onsite Test (full version) held in the large auditorium, Jadwin Hall A10.
  • 4:05 - 4:45 pm: The individual Onsite Test (short version) held in Jadwin Hall A07.
  • 4:00 - 6:00 pm: The Judging process occurs, at which point participants are free to talk to current students in the Physics Department volunteering at the event to learn more about day-to-day life of a Princeton undergrad concentrating in physics (or a related discipline).
  • 6:30 pm (contestants are highly encouraged to come earlier): the Awards Ceremony begins in Jadwin A10, to conclude by 7:00 pm.

    Directions to campus

  • Please read this overview for traveling to campus from various directions.
  • Please note that there will be no parking available in the lots on Ivy Lane or LOT 21. Per official instructions, on 11/22/2014 guests may park in LOT 10, 13, or the North Garage only. For more details, the parking map is located here.
  • Per university policy, high school students are not permitted to stay overnight on campus.
