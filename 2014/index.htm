Welcome!

The inaugural Princeton University Physics Competition will be held in November 2014. Its aim is to provide high school students with a venue to exhibit their skills in solving non-ordinary physics problems, learn new concepts from present-day areas of physics undergoing groundbreaking research, while at the same time competing for awards (plaques for best teams, medals for individual finalists, and certificates for all Onsite participants).

The competition will consist of two separate parts: the team-based Online Part, released to registered participants through e-mail on November 15 and due by November 23, and the individual-based Onsite Test, which is to be taken in Jadwin Hall, housing the Physics Department at Princeton University, in the afternoon of November 22.

  • The Online Part will present material on two different topics in physics and will ask contestants to both answer theoretical questions and perform calculations on real data relevant to the material at hand. In this way, the purpose of the Online Part is to introduce contestants to what it means to perform real research in a "burning" topic in physics.
    Detailed guidelines and sample problems for what may be expected on the Online part are now available for download here.

  • The Onsite Test will contain approximately five challenging problems that contestants are to ponder on for the duration of the testing time. Partial credit will be awarded for any attempt at a problem, so we encourage everyone to try their best and not be afraid of tackling a problem that may seem difficult. The purpose of the Onsite Test is to develop contestants' inner intuition of the beauty of physics.
    Sample problems for the Onsite Test are now available for download here.

    The event is organized and run entirely by undergraduates affiliated with the Princeton Society of Physics Students. Please see the recruitment flyer if you would like to distribute information about this event among interested students. Participants may register here before November 15, 2014.
